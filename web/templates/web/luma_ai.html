Luma AI: Photorealistic Video Generation and 3D Capture

Luma AI builds tools for photorealistic AI content—Dream Machine for text-to-video generation, and NeRF-based 3D capture that turns phone videos into interactive 3D scenes used in games, AR, and film production.

What is Luma AI?

Luma AI is a San Francisco AI company that builds photorealistic content generation tools. It is best known for two things: Dream Machine—a text-to-video and image-to-video generator—and its NeRF (Neural Radiance Field) capture technology, which converts ordinary phone video into high-quality 3D scenes.

Dream Machine is Luma's answer to RunwayML and Pika, with a strong emphasis on physical realism—objects move with weight, light behaves naturally, and camera motions feel grounded. The 3D capture tools serve a different, more technical audience: game developers, VFX artists, and e-commerce teams who need realistic 3D assets.

Key features of Luma AI

1. Dream Machine — text-to-video

Generate photorealistic video clips from text prompts. Dream Machine is particularly strong on physical plausibility—how objects move, how light interacts with surfaces—compared to competing generators.

2. Image-to-video

Upload a still image as the first (and optionally last) frame, and Dream Machine generates a video that interpolates between them—useful for precise creative control over start and end states.

3. Keyframe control (first and last frame)

Set both a starting and ending image and let Dream Machine generate the transition—giving filmmakers a way to plan exactly how a shot begins and ends.

4. 3D Capture (NeRF)

Walk around an object recording with your phone and Luma converts the footage into a photorealistic, interactive 3D scene using Neural Radiance Fields. No expensive scanning equipment needed.

5. Genie — 3D generation from text

Genie generates textured 3D objects from text prompts—useful for rapid 3D asset creation in game development and AR without manual modelling.

6. API for developers

Luma's Dream Machine API allows developers to integrate text-to-video generation into their own applications programmatically.

How Luma AI works

Dream Machine is built on a diffusion-based video model trained to understand physical world dynamics. The 3D capture pipeline uses NeRF—a technique that learns a continuous 3D representation of a scene from 2D images by modelling how light rays pass through space.

  • For video: enter a prompt (or upload images), choose duration and aspect ratio, generate
  • For 3D capture: record a 360° walk around an object with your phone, upload to the Luma app, wait for processing
  • For Genie: describe a 3D object in text and download the generated mesh

Pros and cons of Luma AI

Pros

  • Strong physical realism in video generation
  • First/last frame control for precise storytelling
  • NeRF 3D capture is genuinely professional quality
  • API available for application integration
  • Unique combination of video and 3D tooling

Cons

  • Free tier is limited—serious use requires a paid plan
  • 3D capture requires a careful, methodical recording technique
  • Dream Machine clips are 5–10 seconds—short for storytelling
  • Smaller community than RunwayML or Pika

Who should use Luma AI?

Bonus section

Tips for great 3D captures

  • Move slowly and steadily around the object—fast movements cause blur artefacts
  • Ensure even lighting: avoid strong directional shadows; diffuse daylight or a lightbox works best
  • Overlap your passes: multiple loops from different heights produce better geometry reconstruction

Tips for Dream Machine prompts

  • Describe physics: "water splashing," "cloth billowing in wind" leverages Dream Machine's physical realism strengths
  • Use keyframes: setting a first and last image gives you far more narrative control than text alone
