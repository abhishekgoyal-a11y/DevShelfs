RunwayML: AI Video, Effects, and Images in the Browser

RunwayML bundles generative models for text-to-video, intelligent edits, background work, and stills—so you can ship social clips, prototypes, and concepts without mastering a traditional NLE first.

Open Runway app ↗ Visit runwayml.com ↗

What is RunwayML?

RunwayML is a tool that uses artificial intelligence to help you create videos, images, and visual effects—without needing advanced technical skills.

Think of it like a creative assistant that can:

  • Turn text into videos
  • Remove backgrounds from clips
  • Generate visuals from simple ideas

If you have ever wished you could edit videos or create cool visuals without learning complicated software, RunwayML is designed exactly for that.

Key features of RunwayML

Here are some of the standout things you can do with RunwayML:

Text-to-video

Type a sentence, and it creates a video based on your idea. It feels a bit like magic the first time you try it.

AI video editing

You can:

  • Remove objects from videos
  • Change backgrounds
  • Add effects automatically

Image generation

Describe an image, and RunwayML generates it for you.

Green screen without a green screen

No need for special setups—it can remove backgrounds using AI.

Fast and browser-based

You do not need a powerful computer. It works right in your browser.

How RunwayML works (explained simply)

At its core, RunwayML uses trained AI models.

Here is a simple way to understand it:

  1. You give it an input (text, image, or video)
  2. The AI understands your input based on patterns it learned from tons of data
  3. It generates or edits content based on that input

Example:

  • You type: “A dog running on the moon”
  • RunwayML creates a short video showing that idea

You do not need to know how the AI works behind the scenes—it is all handled for you.

Bonus section

Practical tips for beginners

  • Start with simple prompts (do not overcomplicate things)
  • Experiment a lot—AI improves with trial and error
  • Save your best outputs for reuse
  • Watch what others are creating for inspiration

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Expecting perfect results on the first try
  • Using vague prompts like “make something cool”
  • Ignoring lighting, style, or mood in prompts
  • Burning through credits too quickly without testing
