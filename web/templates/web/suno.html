Suno: Generate Complete Songs with Vocals from a Text Prompt

Suno is an AI music generation tool that turns a text description into a full song—complete with lyrics, vocals, melody, and instrumentation—in seconds, across virtually any genre.

What is Suno?

Suno is an AI music generation platform that creates complete, polished songs from text prompts. Unlike tools that generate instrumentals or short clips, Suno produces tracks with lead vocals, backing harmonies, full instrumentation, and coherent structure—verse, chorus, bridge—all from a single description like "an upbeat indie pop song about missing the ocean."

It is the closest thing available to having a full music production studio that responds to plain English requests.

Key features of Suno

1. Text-to-song generation

Describe the style, mood, and theme in a prompt and Suno returns a full song—typically 2–4 minutes—with vocals and full production. No musical knowledge required.

2. Custom lyrics mode

Write your own lyrics and Suno will compose the music and vocal performance around them—giving you creative control over the words while the AI handles everything else.

3. Wide genre coverage

From lo-fi hip-hop to death metal to baroque classical to K-pop—Suno handles an extraordinary range of styles, and you can blend genres in a single prompt.

4. Song extension and continuation

Take an existing clip and extend it—add a bridge, an outro, or a completely new section—keeping the same style and sonic identity as the original.

5. Stems separation (Pro)

Pro users can download separated audio stems—vocals, drums, bass, and instruments—for use in DAWs and further production work.

6. Community and sharing

Suno has a built-in feed where users share their creations—a great source of inspiration for what prompts work well across different styles.

How Suno works

  • You write a prompt describing the song (style, mood, theme, era)
  • Optionally add your own lyrics or let Suno generate them
  • Suno generates two song variants for you to compare
  • Download the full audio, extend sections, or remix further

Good prompt formula: [tempo/energy] + [genre] + [instruments] + [theme/mood] — e.g. "slow, melancholic jazz piano ballad about watching a city at night."

Pros and cons of Suno

Pros

  • Full songs with vocals—not just instrumentals
  • Incredibly fast generation (under 30 seconds)
  • No musical knowledge needed
  • Huge genre range
  • Generous free tier for exploration

Cons

  • Limited control over specific musical elements
  • Lyrics can be generic or nonsensical on complex topics
  • Commercial licensing requires a paid plan
  • Ongoing legal questions around training data

Suno pricing

  • Free — 50 credits/day, non-commercial use only
  • Pro ($8/month) — 2,500 credits/month, commercial rights, priority generation
  • Premier ($24/month) — 10,000 credits/month, commercial rights, stems download

Alternatives to Suno

  • Udio — similar full-song AI with a slightly different sound signature
  • Mubert — continuous AI music streaming for content creators
  • Soundraw — AI music with more manual control over arrangement
  • ElevenLabs (Sound Effects) — for audio effects rather than full music tracks

Who should use Suno?

  • Content creators who need royalty-free music at scale
  • Songwriters who want to prototype ideas quickly
  • Developers and marketers building audio branding
  • Anyone who wants to create music but lacks formal training

Bonus section

Prompt tips for better songs

  • Use style tags: wrap genre and mood words in square brackets like [melancholic indie folk] to guide the model more reliably
  • Specify era: "80s synth-pop" or "90s grunge" produces more consistent results than just a genre name
  • Generate multiple versions: Suno produces two variants per prompt—always compare before downloading
  • Use Custom Mode for lyrics: Suno's auto-lyrics are decent but your own words always produce more meaningful songs

Suno makes music creation as accessible as writing a text message—it is one of those tools that genuinely surprises you the first time you use it.
