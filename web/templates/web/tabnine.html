What is Tabnine?

Tabnine has been in the AI coding game since 2019 — long before it was a trend. It's one of the earliest and most battle-tested AI code completion tools, and it's carved out a unique niche: privacy-first, enterprise-ready AI assistance that can run entirely on your own hardware.

While tools like GitHub Copilot send your code to the cloud to generate suggestions, Tabnine offers a local model option that runs entirely on your machine. Your code stays on your machine, period. For enterprises with strict IP policies, regulated industries, or just developers who care about data sovereignty, this is a genuinely important distinction.

Beyond privacy, Tabnine learns from your team's codebase patterns — so the more your team uses it, the more it sounds like your codebase, not generic training data.

Key features of Tabnine

Smart code completions

Single-line and whole-function completions that understand the context of what you're building. It considers your current file, recent edits, and imported modules — not just the line you're typing.

Local model (privacy mode)

Tabnine's local model runs entirely offline on your hardware. No code is ever transmitted anywhere. This is Tabnine's biggest differentiator — and for many enterprise customers, the deciding factor.

Protected AI (no training on your code)

Even when using the cloud model, Tabnine guarantees your code is never used to train the general model. What you write stays yours. This commitment is backed contractually for enterprise customers.

Personalized AI for your team

Tabnine can learn from your team's codebase — your patterns, your naming conventions, your internal APIs — without sending that data to train a shared model. It gets smarter about your specific project over time.

AI Chat

Ask questions, generate functions from descriptions, explain legacy code, and get refactoring suggestions in a conversational interface directly inside your IDE.

Broad IDE support

VS Code, JetBrains (IntelliJ, PyCharm, WebStorm, etc.), Vim, Neovim, Eclipse, Emacs, and more. Works across the full range of languages: Python, JavaScript, Java, Go, C++, Ruby, and dozens more.

How Tabnine works, simply

Tabnine runs in two modes, and the choice is yours:

  • Cloud mode: Your code context is sent to Tabnine's servers, predictions are generated by a powerful cloud model, and suggestions stream back to your editor. Fast, high quality, but data leaves your machine.
  • Local mode: A smaller model runs entirely on your CPU or GPU. No data ever leaves. Completions may be slightly simpler, but everything is private.

Either way, Tabnine watches what you type, builds up a picture of your coding context (current file, open files, recent edits), and predicts the next most likely code block. The longer you use it, the more it adapts to your style — and in team mode, to your whole team's patterns.

Alternatives to Tabnine

  • GitHub Copilot — more powerful completions, better chat, wider community. But no local model and your code does go to the cloud.
  • Codeium — strong free tier, good completions, and a competitive alternative for individuals who don't need the enterprise privacy features.

  • Continue (open source) — a fully open-source, self-hosted AI coding assistant that lets you connect your own models. Maximum privacy control.

Who should use Tabnine?

Tabnine is the right choice if you:

  • Work in an industry with strict data privacy requirements (finance, healthcare, legal, government)
  • Need a contractual guarantee that your code won't be used to train public AI models
  • Want an AI that learns your team's specific coding patterns and internal APIs
  • Need on-premises deployment in an air-gapped environment
  • Care deeply about keeping proprietary code off third-party servers

If privacy and enterprise controls aren't your top concerns, Codeium (free) or GitHub Copilot (more powerful) might be better fits. Tabnine's sweet spot is the team or enterprise that won't compromise on data sovereignty.

Bonus: tips and mistakes to avoid

Tips for getting the most out of Tabnine

  • Give it time to learn. Tabnine's personalized suggestions improve the more you use it in your project. Don't judge it after day one.
  • Set up team sharing for enterprise. The more team members use Tabnine in a shared codebase, the smarter it gets about your patterns.
  • Try the local model for sensitive files. Even on a paid plan, you can switch to local mode for specific sensitive files while keeping cloud mode elsewhere.

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Evaluating it on the free tier alone. The free tier is intentionally limited. If you're evaluating for a team, use the trial of the paid plan instead.
  • Expecting GPT-4 quality from the local model. The local model is fast and private, but it's smaller. It trades raw power for privacy.
  • Skipping the codebase indexing setup. For the personalized features to work, you need to properly configure which repositories Tabnine should learn from.

Tabnine isn't trying to win on buzz — it's trying to win on trust. For teams where code privacy is non-negotiable, it's one of the few AI tools that takes that requirement seriously from the ground up.
