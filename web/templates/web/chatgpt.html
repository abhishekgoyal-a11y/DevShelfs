ChatGPT: The AI Assistant That Started It All

ChatGPT is OpenAI's conversational AI assistant — write, code, research, brainstorm, and get things done by having a natural back-and-forth conversation with one of the most capable AI models in the world.

What is ChatGPT?

ChatGPT is an AI chatbot made by OpenAI. When it launched in November 2022, it went from zero to one million users in just five days — faster than any app in history. That kind of reception doesn't happen by accident. People immediately recognized that this was something genuinely different.

At its core, ChatGPT lets you have a conversation with an AI that can write, explain, code, summarize, translate, brainstorm, and reason through problems. You type a message, it responds. You push back, ask follow-up questions, or refine the direction — and it adapts. It's less like a search engine and more like having a very well-read collaborator available 24/7.

Today ChatGPT supports text, images, files, voice, and browsing. It runs on OpenAI's most capable models and has an ecosystem of custom GPTs built by third parties. It's both beginner-friendly and deep enough for professional workflows.

Key features of ChatGPT

Natural conversation

Ask questions, get answers, push back, refine — it keeps the full context of your conversation so you don't have to repeat yourself.

Writing assistance

Draft emails, essays, blog posts, cover letters, scripts, or social captions. You give the brief; ChatGPT gives you a strong first draft.

Code generation & debugging

Write code in any major language, fix bugs, explain what existing code does, or get step-by-step help learning a new framework.

Image understanding & generation

Upload an image and ask questions about it. Or generate images using DALL·E directly inside the chat. Great for visual thinkers.

Web browsing

On paid plans, ChatGPT can search the internet and pull in current information — so you're not limited to its training data.

Custom GPTs

Thousands of specialized AI assistants built on top of ChatGPT — for cooking, law, fitness, coding, language learning, and more. Many are free to use.

Voice mode

Have a spoken back-and-forth conversation with ChatGPT on mobile. It listens, responds in natural speech, and can be interrupted mid-sentence.

How ChatGPT works

ChatGPT is powered by large language models (LLMs) — specifically OpenAI's GPT series. Here's the simple version of what happens every time you send a message:

  • It reads everything in your conversation — every message, in order, every time.
  • It predicts the most useful next response — not by looking things up in a database, but by pattern-matching across everything it learned during training.
  • It generates text word by word — which is why you sometimes see it "think out loud" before arriving at an answer.

The model was trained on a huge portion of the internet, books, and other text sources — giving it a broad base of knowledge. Then it was fine-tuned to be helpful, honest, and safe through a process called RLHF (reinforcement learning from human feedback), where human raters guided it toward better answers.

Pros and cons of ChatGPT

Pros

  • Extremely versatile — writing, coding, analysis, image, voice
  • Huge ecosystem of custom GPTs for specialized tasks
  • Free tier is generous and genuinely capable
  • Constantly updated with new features and models
  • Excellent at maintaining context in long conversations
  • Works in dozens of languages

Cons

  • Can confidently state wrong information (hallucinations)
  • Free plan doesn't browse the web in real time
  • Responses can be verbose and over-explain
  • No persistent memory by default across sessions
  • Web browsing results are less citation-focused than Perplexity

ChatGPT pricing

  • Free — access to GPT-4o with limits, basic image generation, and voice mode. Solid for casual use.
  • Plus — $20/month — higher limits on GPT-4o, web browsing, more image generation, and early access to new features.
  • Team — $25/user/month — everything in Plus, with shared workspaces and admin controls for small teams.
  • Enterprise — custom pricing for large organisations. Includes advanced security, unlimited context, and dedicated support.

Tip: OpenAI frequently adjusts what's included in each tier. Check openai.com/chatgpt/pricing for the latest.

Alternatives to ChatGPT

  • Claude (Anthropic) — better for long documents and nuanced writing; very thoughtful responses with a large context window.
  • Google Gemini — deeply integrated with Google Docs, Gmail, and Google Search; great if you live in Google's ecosystem.
  • Perplexity AI — best for real-time web research with cited sources; less conversational but more verifiable.
  • Microsoft Copilot — ChatGPT-powered but built into Bing and Microsoft 365; free for basic use.

Who should use ChatGPT?

ChatGPT is a strong fit if you:

  • Want one AI tool that handles writing, coding, research, and conversation
  • Are a developer, student, writer, or business professional
  • Need a tool that supports files, images, and voice — not just text
  • Want access to a large library of specialized AI assistants (custom GPTs)
  • Are comfortable verifying important outputs before acting on them

If your primary need is real-time, sourced research, Perplexity may suit you better. If you want the best-in-class long-document assistant, Claude is worth a look. But for sheer breadth? ChatGPT is still the benchmark.

Bonus section

Practical tips for beginners

  • Give it a role. "You are an experienced copywriter. Write me…" gets better results than just "Write me…"
  • Iterate, don't restart. Say "make it shorter," "add more examples," or "rewrite the intro" — don't start a new chat.
  • Use custom GPTs. Browse the GPT Store for tools tuned to your specific use case — many are better than the base model for niche tasks.
  • Upload files for context. Paste your notes, a PDF, or a chunk of code and ask ChatGPT to work from your material.

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Copying without checking. ChatGPT can fabricate facts, names, and citations. Always verify before publishing or acting.
  • Asking everything in one go. Break complex requests into steps — the output quality improves significantly.
  • Ignoring the system prompt. If you're building workflows, a clear instruction at the start of a conversation sets the tone for everything after.
  • Forgetting it has no memory by default. Each new chat starts fresh — paste in your context or use Projects/Memory features if you need continuity.

ChatGPT kicked off the AI revolution that's still accelerating. Whether you're writing your first email draft with AI or building complex multi-step workflows, it remains one of the most capable and accessible places to start.
